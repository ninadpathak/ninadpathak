# Measuring Content ROI for B2B SaaS

Page views do not pay salaries. Social shares do not close deals. You need metrics that connect content to revenue.

Here is how to measure content ROI for B2B SaaS.

## Track Content Touchpoints in Your CRM

Add a field to your CRM for content touchpoints. Log which blog posts prospects read. Track which tutorials they complete. Note which whitepapers they download.

When a deal closes, attribute revenue to those touchpoints. This shows which content assists conversions.

## Measure Pipeline Velocity

Compare sales cycles for prospects who engage with content versus those who do not. Content should educate buyers. Educated buyers make decisions faster.

Track time from first touch to closed won. Segment by content engagement level. Look for correlation.

## Calculate Customer Acquisition Cost by Channel

Divide content marketing spend by customers acquired through content. Include writer salaries. Include design costs. Include promotion budgets.

Compare this CAC to paid acquisition. Content CAC should decrease over time as evergreen posts continue generating leads.

## Survey Sales Teams

Ask your sales team which content they use. Which posts do they send to prospects? Which case studies close deals?

Sales teams know what works. Their feedback identifies high value content that metrics might miss.

## Track Product Qualified Leads

Measure how many readers take product actions after reading content. Do they sign up for trials? Do they request demos? Do they install SDKs?

Product actions indicate content relevance. Browsers read and leave. Buyers read and try.

## Monitor Retention Correlation

Analyze whether customers who read your content during evaluation stay longer. Do they have higher NPS scores? Do they expand usage faster?

Content sets expectations. Accurate content attracts customers who fit your product.

## Report on Revenue Attribution

Build a monthly report showing content influence on revenue. Include pipeline influenced. Include deals sourced. Include average deal size for content touched opportunities.

Present this to leadership. Speak their language. Show dollars, not downloads.

---

Content ROI measurement takes work. It requires CRM discipline. It requires patience. It requires focusing on business outcomes over vanity metrics.

The effort pays off. You will know which content to produce. You will know which content to kill. You will prove the value of your content investment.
