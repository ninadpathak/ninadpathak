The First 90 Days of a DevTools Content Strategy

Your first three months set the trajectory. Focus on quick wins, technical foundation, and building distribution before chasing perfection.

You joined a DevTools company. You need to build content from nothing. Three months feels like nothing. Three months is enough.

Here is your 90 day roadmap.

Days 1 to 30: Foundation

Week 1: Research Interview five customers. Understand why they chose your product. Interview five churned users. Understand why they left.

Study your search console data. See what queries bring traffic. Study competitor content. Find gaps they ignore.

Week 2: Audit List every existing content asset. Score each on traffic, accuracy, and alignment with current positioning.

Identify the top ten posts needing updates. Identify gaps where no content exists for key use cases.

Week 3: Infrastructure Set up your content calendar. Define your editorial workflow. Create templates for common content types.

Document your style guide. Cover tone, code formatting, and review processes.

Week 4: Quick Wins Update your top five outdated posts. Fix broken links. Improve meta descriptions. Add internal links.

Publish your first new piece. Choose a topic your research identified as high priority.

Days 31 to 60: Momentum

Week 5 and 6: Core Content Create your definitive guide to your primary use case. This becomes your cornerstone content.

Write three tutorials covering common workflows. Include working code. Include screenshots.

Week 7: Distribution Setup Establish your newsletter. Create templates. Build your initial subscriber list from existing contacts.

Identify three communities where your audience gathers. Reddit. Discord. Slack groups. Prepare to contribute genuinely.

Week 8: Launch and Learn Ship your cornerstone guide. Promote it through your channels. Monitor performance closely.

Survey readers. Ask what confused them. Ask what they want next.

Days 61 to 90: Scale

Week 9 and 10: Systematize Document what worked. Create templates based on your successful posts. Build a repeatable process.

Hire freelancers for execution. You focus on strategy and final review.

Week 11: Expand Launch a second content series. Choose a different format. Video tutorials. Interactive playgrounds. Podcast interviews.

Test whether your audience prefers different mediums.

Week 12: Measure and Report Compile metrics for the quarter. Traffic growth. Engagement rates. Conversion events.

Present results to leadership. Show what content cost. Show what it produced.

Your first 90 days establish patterns. Move fast. Ship frequently. Learn from real audience response. Perfection comes through iteration, not planning.
