The 30 Day Content Refresh Framework

Old blog posts decay. Screenshots become outdated. Links break. Code examples use deprecated syntax. Traffic drops.

You have two options. Write new posts. Or fix the old ones.

Refreshing old content takes less time and delivers faster results. Here is a framework for doing it in 30 days.

Week 1: Audit and Prioritize

Pull a report of your top 50 posts by traffic. Look at posts ranking on page two of search results. Find posts with high impressions but low click through rates.

Score each post on three factors: * Traffic potential (search volume for the target keyword) * Current ranking (positions 5 to 15 are easiest to improve) * Update complexity (how much needs to change)

Pick 10 posts to refresh. Focus on those with high potential and medium complexity.

Week 2: Update the Content

Work through your 10 posts systematically.

Update code examples to current syntax. Replace old screenshots. Fix broken links. Add new sections that answer related questions.

Expand thin content. Posts under 800 words rarely rank well. Add depth where needed.

Check the search intent. Does your post match what people actually want? Adjust the angle if necessary.

Week 3: Improve On Page SEO

Rewrite title tags to include target keywords naturally. Update meta descriptions to improve click through rates.

Add internal links from newer posts. Link to your refreshed posts from relevant pages.

Improve headers. Use descriptive H2s and H3s that include variations of your target keywords.

Add schema markup where appropriate. FAQ schema, how to schema, and code schema help visibility.

Week 4: Promote and Monitor

Republish the refreshed posts with current dates. Share them on social media. Include them in your newsletter.

Submit updated URLs to Google Search Console. Request indexing for major changes.

Set up tracking. Monitor rankings weekly. Watch traffic changes. Note which updates worked.

Build a Refresh Calendar

Refreshing content is never done. Create a recurring calendar.

Review top posts quarterly. Refresh declining posts every six months. Archive posts that no longer fit your positioning.

Document what works. Some updates double traffic. Others do nothing. Learn the patterns.

Content refresh is compound interest for your blog. Small improvements stack. Old posts become new assets. Start your first 30 day cycle this week.
